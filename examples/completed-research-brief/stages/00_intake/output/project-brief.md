# Project Brief

## Project Name

Research Brief Example

## Desired Outcome

Create a project-specific ICM workspace for turning messy research notes, source links, and stakeholder questions into a concise decision-ready research brief.

## Starting Material

- Research questions from a product or strategy lead
- Source links, transcripts, notes, and prior internal decisions
- Known constraints around audience, deadline, and evidence quality

## Audience Or Users

- Primary user: a researcher or analyst preparing the brief
- Reviewer: a stakeholder who needs to trust the evidence chain
- Final audience: decision-makers who need a clear recommendation

## Success Criteria

- The workflow separates intake, source triage, synthesis, brief drafting, and validation.
- Every major claim in the final brief traces to a source, decision, or stated assumption.
- A human can review source quality before the final recommendation is written.
- Repeated evidence-quality rules can move into stable reference files.

## Constraints

- Time: fit a two-to-three day research sprint
- Budget: use markdown and local scripts before adding specialized tooling
- Tools: markdown, git, and coding-agent workflows
- Privacy or compliance: do not expose private interview notes in public-ready outputs
- Must avoid: unsourced recommendations and hidden assumptions

## Natural Review Points

- After source triage
- After synthesis themes are selected
- Before the final brief is accepted

## Open Questions

- Which source types should count as primary evidence?
- Should confidence be scored numerically or with low/medium/high labels?
- What minimum traceability should be required before a recommendation is accepted?
