# Project Brief

## Project Name

Project Plan Example

## Desired Outcome

Create a project-specific ICM workspace for planning a small cross-functional initiative without losing source traceability, milestone clarity, or sponsor review control.

## Starting Material

- A rough initiative idea from a project sponsor.
- Current roadmap notes and known constraints.
- A few stakeholder requests and open risks.
- Existing team availability and target delivery window.

## Audience Or Users

- Primary user: project owner turning a rough initiative into an executable plan.
- Secondary user: contributors who need clear tasks, owners, and dates.
- Reviewer: sponsor checking scope, risk, and go/no-go readiness.

## Success Criteria

- Every major milestone traces to a source, constraint, or accepted decision.
- The plan separates scope, milestones, owners, risks, and open questions.
- The final project calendar uses clear dates and owner/status fields.
- A human can approve, request changes, or block the plan before work starts.

## Constraints

- Time: initial plan should be drafted in one focused planning pass.
- Budget: no paid planning platform required.
- Tools: markdown, local CLI checks, git, and coding-agent workflows.
- Privacy or compliance: summarize private stakeholder notes without quoting sensitive details.
- Must avoid: hidden assumptions, unowned milestones, ambiguous dates, and scope commitments without sponsor review.

## Natural Review Points

- After source and constraint inventory.
- After milestone map.
- Before accepting owners and dates.
- Before starting implementation.

## Open Questions

- Which constraints are hard blockers versus preferences?
- Who has final decision authority if timeline and scope conflict?
- What minimum source coverage should be required before a project plan is approved?
